1. Overview

Eavora may use cookies, pixels, tags, local storage, session storage, analytics scripts, and similar technologies to operate the website, support form functionality, improve user experience, and understand how visitors interact with the platform.

This Cookie Policy should be read together with the Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

2. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. Cookies can help websites remember preferences, keep features working, improve security, measure performance, and understand general usage patterns.

Similar technologies may work in a related way, even if they are not technically cookies. For simplicity, this policy refers to cookies and similar technologies together as “cookies.”

3. Types of Cookies We May Use

Essential cookies

These cookies help the website load, display content, remember cookie banner choices, process form interactions, and maintain basic security.

Analytics cookies

These cookies may help understand page visits, traffic sources, user interaction patterns, and website performance.

Performance cookies

These cookies may help identify loading issues, technical problems, or areas where the website experience can be improved.

Marketing cookies

These cookies may help measure advertising activity, understand campaign performance, or support relevant website interactions where enabled.

4. How Cookies May Be Used

Eavora may use cookies to:

  • Remember cookie banner choices
  • Support website navigation and form functionality
  • Improve page speed, reliability, and user experience
  • Measure website traffic and page engagement
  • Understand which service categories users view
  • Help protect the website from spam, misuse, or suspicious activity
  • Support analytics, reporting, or advertising measurement where enabled

5. Third-Party Cookies and Tools

Some cookies or similar technologies may be placed by third-party tools, such as analytics providers, advertising platforms, hosting providers, security tools, embedded scripts, or performance monitoring services.

These third parties may process information according to their own privacy policies and cookie practices. Eavora does not control every third-party cookie or tracking technology once it is provided by an external service.

6. Cookie Banner Choices

The website may display a cookie banner or notice that allows users to accept or acknowledge cookie use. If a cookie preference feature is enabled, your selection may be stored so the banner does not appear repeatedly.

Essential cookies may still be used because they are needed for basic website operation, security, and form functionality.

7. Your Browser Choices

Most browsers allow you to block, delete, or manage cookies through browser settings. Your options may vary depending on the browser and device you use.

If you block or delete cookies, some website features may not work correctly. For example, the cookie banner may reappear, form features may behave differently, or certain analytics and preference features may be unavailable.

8. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer “Do Not Track” or similar signals. Because there is no consistent industry standard for responding to these signals, the website may not respond to them in a specific way.

9. Relationship to Personal Information

Some cookie-related data may be considered personal information depending on your location and applicable law. For more information about how Eavora may collect, use, and share information, please review the Privacy Policy.
